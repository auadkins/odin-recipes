Spaghet!!

How about spooning a helping of Spaghet down your throat?

Fill that sauce into my gullet!

For many of us, our childhoods memories are defined by Thursday night Spaghet.

We always knew it was there, lurking somewhere amongst the shrubbery, waiting to spook us...

with it's deliciousness.

Though scholars maintain that this recipe was lost decades ago, our team managed to recreate it

Follow along in your own kitchen as we bring Spaghet back to dinner this Thursday!

Just remember - do not serve this dish within 500 ft of an elementary school.

Ingredients

  • Third Grade reading level
  • 52 year old body
  • Poorly implanted hair plugs
  • Form fitting spandex turtleneck
  • Enough Ragu to allow it to spill seductively from thin lips for at least 45 minutes
  • Some noodles. Pretty sure it doesn't matter what kind. Just use the same ones you used for the lasagna
  • Roughly 35 gallons of whole milk to boil the noodles in
  • A bath tub. To boil the noodles in

Steps

  1. Put on a few extra layers of clothes to ward of wandering Spaghet hands
  2. Ensure that the children are at a babysitter's, preferably out of town. Do NOT tell Spaghet where they are
  3. Dump the milk into the bathtub
  4. Dump the noodles into the bathtub
  5. Dump the Spaghet into the bathtub
  6. Allow Spaghet's natural sensuality to heat the tub until the noodles boil
  7. Let the noodles simmer for roughly two hours
  8. Use a pool skimmer to transfer the noodles to your plates
  9. Siphon the milk into a pitcher to serve dinner drinks
  10. See if there is any sauce leftover from the lasagna. If so, add a hearty drizzle to each plate. If none is leftover, repeat steps 1-7 from the lasagna recipe
  11. Consume the Spaghet (meal), ensuring that Spaghet (the man) is seated outside arm's reach
